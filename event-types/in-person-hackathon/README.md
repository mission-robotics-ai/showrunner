# In-person hackathon

The full scaffold for hosting a hands-on hackathon — built from running a 3-day robotics hackathon (~70–80 hackers, ~12 teams, real hardware on the floor) and distilled so you can plan yours in an afternoon.

## The planning sequence that works

1. **Lock date + venue + the hardware question** (what do teams build on, who supplies it) — everything else derives from these
2. **Copy a run-of-show skeleton** into your planning doc; adjust clock times to your venue
3. **Recruit judges early** — they go in the announcement, so their names amplify registration
4. **Send partners the run-of-show email** ([template](../../templates/partner-run-of-show-email.md)) + open the partner Slack channel ([template](../../templates/partner-slack-channel.md)) the week of
5. **Before doors, assign the four named owners:** food (with card + budget), photo/video, submissions/results, partner channel
6. **Day 0 checklist:** hardware tested, scoring sheets ready to print, fulfillment sheet created, submission-form notifications verified reaching a monitored inbox

## The four leaks every hackathon springs without owners

The event itself will go fine. What leaks is the value after: **photos** nobody took, **results** nobody wrote down, **thanks** nobody sent inside the warm window, and **commitments** (sponsor deliverables, loaner returns, prize wires) nobody tracked. Each gets a named owner before doors open, or you'll reconstruct them afterward from receipts and memory. Ask us how we know.
