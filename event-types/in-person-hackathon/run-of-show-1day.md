# Run of show — 1-day hackathon (9am–6pm) — PROPOSED SCAFFOLD

A single-day compression of the 3-day format. This one is a proposal, not yet field-tested — the beats derive from what worked across three days, compressed by the ratios below. Drop it into your planning doc and build from there.

## The compression logic

A one-day event can't train overnight, so the whole design shifts: **shorter iteration loops, earlier pivot deadline, simpler judging.** Protect build time above everything — ceremony is the first thing you cut, not the last. Target ratio: ~6 hours building out of 9 on site.

## The day

- **8:15am — Staff + partners in.** Hardware powered and tested before any attendee arrives. (This inherits the whole job of the 3-day format's load-in day — compressed to 45 minutes, which only works if partners tested their gear the day before. If you can get hardware in the night before, do.)
- **9:00am — Doors + breakfast.**
- **9:30am — Kickoff + bootcamp-lite (30 min, hands-on).** *Same first-pick-of-hardware hook as the 3-day: attendees who make the 9:30 bootcamp choose robots first.*
- **10:00am — Team formation + hardware claim.** *Hard 15 minutes. Teams that dither here spend the day negotiating instead of building.*
- **10:15am — Build.**
- **12:30pm — Lunch lands, working.** *No program over lunch. Feed them where they sit.*
- **2:00pm — Mid-day checkpoint (15 min, one organizer on mic).** *This replaces tech talks AND serves as the pivot deadline: any team that can't demo its core loop by 2pm should be told, kindly and directly, to simplify scope. In the 3-day format the overnight does this triage; in one day, a human has to.*
- **4:30pm — Pencils down. Demos begin.** *3 minutes per team, not 5 — with 10–15 teams this is the difference between demos ending at 5:15 and demos eating the awards.*
- **~5:15pm — Judges deliberate (15 min).** *Score DURING demos on a simple rubric (see judging.md); deliberation is reconciliation, not evaluation. A side-competition final or a highlight reel holds the room.*
- **5:40pm — Winners + closing.**
- **6:00pm — Out. Teardown.**

## What deliberately didn't survive the compression

- **Overnight training** → pivot checkpoint at 2pm does the triage instead
- **Tech talks** → one 15-minute checkpoint; save speakers for a different event
- **Long deliberation** → judges score live during demos
- **Evening social** → breakfast and working lunch carry the mingling; a 6pm hard stop respects everyone's Saturday

## The two capture rules still apply

Photo/video owner named before doors. Results written to the fulfillment sheet before the room empties — on a one-day event there is no "tomorrow" to reconstruct who won what.
