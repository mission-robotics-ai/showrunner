# Food & vendors — in-person hackathon

What we actually ordered for a 3-day robotics hackathon with ~70–80 hackers on the floor, and what we'd tell the next host. Total spend: **just under $3,000, roughly $14 per person per day.** Set your budget at ~$40/person for a full weekend and you'll land close.

## The one structural decision that matters

**Assign a single food owner with a card and a budget.** Ours ran everything on one card against a $3K ceiling, placed all orders in advance on day one, and the hosts never thought about food again all weekend. Food is the easiest workstream to fully delegate — do it. Give the owner this doc.

## Order book (scale: ~70–80 people, 3 days)

All orders placed the Wednesday before; deliveries spread across the event days. Same-day grocery delivery (Instacart/Costco, Safeway) means no truck, no pickup runs, no minimums negotiations.

Exact itemized orders: [orders.md](orders.md)

## Quantities that proved right (per ~75 people)

- **Bagels:** ~1 per person on a morning where everyone arrives; half that on a staggered morning
- **Pizza:** 1 XL per 4–5 people at dinner
- **Group coffee:** 1 box per ~10 people per morning; carafe out by 9am or you'll hear about it
- **Sparkling water:** wildly popular with this crowd — over-order it relative to soda

## Lessons banked

1. **Order confirmations are not receipts.** Instacart/Costco confirmations show a total AND a higher card-authorization hold; the final charge can differ. Reconcile against the card statement, not the confirmation emails.
2. **Keep every receipt in one email thread** from the food owner to whoever holds the books. Our reconciliation took minutes because everything lived in one place.
3. **Buy the serving gear, don't improvise.** $100 of tongs, bowls, and a kettle once — versus scrounging a robotics shop for utensils at 8am.
4. **Alcohol: small and late.** A couple of cases for the kickoff social hour was right. It's a build event; the beer is a signal, not a supply.
