# Judging — in-person hackathon

The judging system that worked, condensed. Paste-ready comms and the day-of ops skeleton live in [`../../templates/judge-comms.md`](../../templates/judge-comms.md) and [`../../templates/judge-ops-plan.md`](../../templates/judge-ops-plan.md).

## The rubric pattern

Four criteria, each scored 1–5:
1. **Works on hardware** — it ran, live, on the real robot
2. **Technical difficulty**
3. **Ingenuity**
4. **Demo quality**

**Give 2× weight to the criterion that defines your event's identity.** Ours was works-on-hardware — a hardware hackathon where the slickest slideware can't beat a robot that actually moves. Ties break on that same criterion.

*Why only four criteria: judges score during and between live demos. A rubric that takes longer to fill than the demo takes to watch will be abandoned by the third team, and you'll get vibes with numbers painted on.*

## The ops beats (3-day format; compress for 1-day)

- Judges on-site for a bounded window (ours: 10am–2pm Sunday) — respect it, they're volunteers
- 15-minute briefing before demos: rubric, timing, one named host per judge
- Demos: ~5 min/team, judges score live on printed sheets (print them the night before)
- Deliberation: 30 min, immediately after demos while everything's fresh
- Winners announced same afternoon

## Lessons banked

1. **Get the rubric signed off days early, not the night before.** Comms to judges gate on it (you can't send "here's how you'll score" until scoring is decided), and the printed sheets gate on it too.
2. **A judging seat promised to a sponsor is a deliverable.** If a sponsor tier includes a panel seat, track it like the wire transfer — an unfilled promised seat is a make-good conversation after the event.
3. **Write results down the moment they're decided** — team names, placements, per-place amounts, into the fulfillment sheet, before the announcement even happens. Post-event copy, prize payouts, and social posts all block on this one row of data, and human memory of "who won what" degrades within a day.
4. **Every judge is a network node.** Confirm their names/photos early enough to put them in the announcement — judges amplify reach before the event, not just evaluate during it. And close their loop after: a same-week thank-you, plus whatever professional courtesy they ask for (reference letters, intros) handled promptly.
