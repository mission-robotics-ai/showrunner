# Prize Fulfillment Sheet — pattern

**How to use:** This describes the shape of the tracker, not the tracker itself — build it as one spreadsheet, one row per prize, sectioned by lane. Start it the moment winners are announced (or sooner, if prize amounts are locked pre-event) and keep it updated until every payout and every piece of hardware has shipped. **This sheet stays PRIVATE.** It holds legal names, home addresses, and phone numbers — never mirror it into public-facing recap docs or social copy.

---

## Structure: one sheet, sections per prize lane

Split the sheet into sections so sponsor-funded and hardware-in-kind prizes don't get lost in the main-track cash rows:

- **Main track** — 1st / 2nd / 3rd (or however the top of the field is split)
- **Sponsor bounties** — per-sponsor cash or credit prizes, tagged to the sponsor funding them
- **Hardware awards** — physical items (boards, kits, devices) with no cash component

## Columns

| Team | Amount | Email | Phone | Full Legal Name | Physical Address | [Compute-platform account, if credits] |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| [Team A] | [$X cash + $Y credit] | [contact email] | [phone] | [legal name for payout/tax purposes] | [mailing address] | [platform username/account ID] |
| [Team B] | [$X] | | | | | |
| [Team C] | [in-kind hardware only] | | | | | n/a |

## Why legal name + physical address matter

Cash and credit prizes above a threshold typically trigger tax reporting obligations (e.g., a 1099 in the US), and physical prizes need a real shipping address. Collect both at the point of winning, not after — teams disperse fast post-event, and a Discord handle or a first-name-only Luma signup is not enough to cut a check or file a tax form. If a compute or SaaS credit is part of the prize, collect the platform account identifier (username or account ID) at the same time so the credit lands on the right account instead of a generic email.

## Reconciliation note

Keep a running total per lane (cash, credits, hardware units) and check it against the publicly-committed prize numbers before any public recap goes out. Sponsor commitments and the fulfillment sheet can drift by the time payouts happen — reconcile explicitly rather than assuming the two match.
