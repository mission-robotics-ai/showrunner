# Partner Slack Channel Kit

**How to use:** Send once partner/sponsor confirmations are locked and you have a venue, dates, and a load-in day. Fill every `[PLACEHOLDER]`, build the invite-list table from your actual sponsor contacts, then create the channel and paste the pinned message as the first post. Send 3-5 days before load-in so partners have time to say who's showing up.

---

## 1. Channel naming pattern

`#[event-name]-partners`

Keep it short and grep-able — this is the channel partners forward to their own team, so the name should read clearly out of context.

## 2. Invite-list table skeleton

Build this before you create the channel. Every gap here is a partner who won't get invited on time.

| Partner | Recipient | Email | Gap / note |
|---|---|---|---|
| [SPONSOR 1] | [Name] | [email] | [e.g. primary contact, already coordinating hardware] |
| [SPONSOR 2] | [Name] | [email] | [e.g. billing contact — confirm before adding to partner threads] |
| [SPONSOR 3] | — | — | **No direct contact on file.** Route through [internal owner] before sending. |
| [SPONSOR 4] | [Name A] + [Name B] | [email] / **missing** | One contact known, one missing — ask the known contact to forward or supply the gap. |

**Gaps to close before sending:**
1. Any sponsor with no named on-site contact — identify the right human before creating the invite, don't guess.
2. Any partner mid-negotiation on a separate thread (deliverables, dollar amounts) — loop the internal owner of that thread so the partner channel doesn't cross wires with the agreement conversation.

## 3. Pinned first message

Paste-ready. Keep the warm, logistics-forward voice and the "drop a wave so I know you're in" close — that's what makes partners actually check the channel instead of muting it.
