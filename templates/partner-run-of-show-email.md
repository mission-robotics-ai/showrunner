# Partner Run-of-Show Email

**How to use:** Send this the day before doors open (or the morning of, if load-in is same-week). One email works for all partners — swap `[Name]` per recipient or send as a small bcc/group. The run of show sits inline so partners can forward it straight to their on-site team without a link click. Fill the `[SLACK-LINK]` placeholder only after the partner Slack channel (see `partner-slack-channel.md`) actually exists.

---

**Subject:** [EVENT NAME] — run of show + your partner Slack channel

**Body:**

Hi [Name]!

Doors open [tomorrow / today], and I could not be more excited to have you in the building for this. Here's everything you and your on-site team need in one place, and I've set up a Slack channel so we can move fast all weekend.

**Our partner Slack channel is live, and it's the fastest line to me.**

I'll post the schedule and any day-of changes there first, so it's the best place to reach me for anything from load-in to a robot acting up on the floor. Join here, and pull in whoever from your team will be on site: [SLACK-LINK]

**Here's the run of show, start to finish.**

Everything happens at [VENUE + ADDRESS]. For building access on any day, ask for [ACCESS CONTACT] at [HOST ORG]. Times are firm unless you hear otherwise from me in Slack.

- *[LOAD-IN DAY]* — Partner load-in and testing opens at [TIME]. [Fill in the windows you'd recommend for setup and testing.]
- *[DAY 1]* — [Fill in: final hardware drop / prep deadline] lands around [TIME], doors open at [TIME], and [kickoff event] begins sharp at [TIME]. [Note any first-pick-of-hardware incentive.] Team formation and kickoff remarks are at [TIME], and [core activity] runs into the night from there.
- *[DAY 2]* — Full build day on the floor, with [any scheduled programming] from [TIME]–[TIME] and [any parallel activity] running in parallel.
- *[DAY 3]* — Pencils down at [TIME], when live demos begin, roughly [N] teams at [X] minutes each. Winners are announced around [TIME], the event wraps at [TIME], and teardown follows.

**A few moments are where your team on the floor matters most.**

If it's useful for planning coverage: [DAY 1] evening is the claim-and-build window, when teams pick hardware and start working, so hardware partners want someone on the floor. [DAY 2] morning is the tooling crunch, when setup and integration questions hit. [DAY 3] morning is demos, when everything has to run, so anyone with hardware in the demos should be present through the judging block.

**One quick thing from you.**

Hop into the Slack channel and let me know who from your team will be on site and which days, so I can make sure they're set up the moment they walk in. Anything you need between now and then, that channel is the place.

Can't wait to build this with you.

- [HOST NAME]

---

**Why keep the three-window framing:** it's the reusable insight, not the event-specific schedule. Every hardware/build event has a claim-and-build window (early, when partners' presence drives adoption), a tooling crunch (mid-event, when integration friction peaks), and a demo/judging window (late, when partners need to see their tech land on stage). Naming these three explicitly is what gets a partner to actually staff the floor instead of just showing up for the kickoff photo.
